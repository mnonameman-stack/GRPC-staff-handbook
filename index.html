1. Introduction & Core Mindset

Welcome to the Grand River Police Department / ERLC Roleplay Community (GRPC) Staff Team. By accepting this rank, you have chosen to take on the responsibility of curating an immersive, safe, and professional environment for hundreds of players. Your role is not to be a dictator, but a facilitator of excellent roleplay.

The ERLC community relies entirely on the consistency and professionalism of its staff members. Players will test your patience, rule-breakers will attempt to manipulate you, and intense roleplay situations will blur the lines of the rules. Your core mindset must always remain analytical, impartial, and calm. You are the definitive authority in the server, meaning you must lead by example. If a staff member loses their temper, the server loses its integrity.

This handbook is your absolute source of truth. Any deviation from these protocols without explicit permission from the Management or Director team may result in internal disciplinary action, up to and including termination and a community blacklist. Read it, understand it, and enforce it.

The following mandates are strictly enforced. Ignorance of these protocols is not a valid defense for violating them.

3. The Modcall Procedure (Step-by-Step)

Handling in-game tickets (Modcalls) is your primary duty. A poorly handled modcall leads to player frustration and accusations of admin abuse. You must follow this standardized procedure for every single ticket you answer, regardless of how minor the issue seems.

Phase 1: Claiming & Assessment

  • Never blind-teleport: Always claim the ticket via the ERLC Modcall UI before engaging. Teleporting to a player without claiming their ticket causes double-staffing and confusion.
  • Read the report thoroughly: Check the context. Is it an active scenario? Are they reporting RDM? Prepare yourself mentally for what you are about to walk into.
  • On-Duty status: Ensure you are completely out of an active roleplay scenario yourself. You cannot moderate a server while actively participating in a bank robbery or traffic stop. If you are deeply invested in a scene, do not claim the ticket.

Phase 2: Professional Engagement

When you teleport to the user, immediately pause the surrounding RP if necessary, or bring the reporting user to an isolated area (like a rooftop or empty field) to avoid disrupting others.

Use a standardized greeting. Do not use slang. Do not type "sup" or "what do u need".

Greetings! I am [Rank] [Your Name] with GRPC Moderation. How can I assist you with your roleplay experience today?

Phase 3: Investigation & Evidence (The "No Clip, No Action" Rule)

Players will frequently lie to get others banned. You are an investigator first and an enforcer second. Listen to the reporting player's claim, then verify it using the server's backend tools.

  • Check the Logs: Use the ERLC command :logs. Check the combat logs to verify who shot first. Check the vehicle logs for VDM. Check the chat logs for toxicity.
  • Requesting Clips: If the situation is a "He-Said/She-Said" scenario (e.g., Fail RP, unrealistic driving, or combat logging) and the in-game logs do not paint a clear picture, you must request a video clip. If the reporting player cannot provide a clip, tell them: "Unfortunately, without video evidence or clear server logs, I cannot issue a punishment at this time. Please use a clipping software like Medal or Shadowplay for future incidents."
  • Isolate the Accused: If evidence is provided, use :bring [username] to bring the accused to your isolated location. Explain the situation cleanly. Example: "You have been brought here due to a report of RDM. The logs confirm you killed [Player] without prior RP engagement."

Phase 4: Resolution & Logging

Once you have confirmed a rule break, issue the punishment strictly aligning with the Punishment Matrix. Do not debate the rules with the rule-breaker. Once you state the verdict, execute the command. After the punishment is issued, return the reporting player to their original location using :return [username].

Finally, you must log the interaction in the designated Discord channel using the required format to ensure your quotas are tracked.

5. Moderation Command Syntax

Using commands correctly is vital. Misusing commands can break the server's immersion or accidentally punish the wrong player. Always double-check usernames.

Communication Commands

  • Private Message: :pm [username] [message] — Used to speak to a specific player without teleporting to them. Good for minor reminders (e.g., ":pm Player123 Please change your avatar, it violates Roblox TOS.").
  • Hint Message: :h [message] — Displays a small banner at the top of the screen. Used for server-wide minor updates (e.g., ":h The server will be restarting in 10 minutes.").
  • Server Message: :m [message] — A massive, screen-blocking message. Use sparingly. Only use this for critical announcements like massive RP events or severe server-wide warnings.

Disciplinary Commands

When issuing a punishment, the reason MUST be detailed. Never put "breaking rules" or "rdm". Follow the standard formatting: [Infraction] | [Brief Detail] | [Your Name].

:warn JohnDoe123 RDM | Shot police at spawn without engagement | Mod Smith
:kick JohnDoe123 VDM (2nd Offense) | Ramming civilian cars at gas station | Mod Smith
:ban JohnDoe123 24h LTAP | Logged out during active pursuit | Mod Smith

6. Exploiter Protocols

Exploiters (hackers) attempt to ruin the server maliciously using third-party software (aimbots, speedhacks, car-flingers, noclip). Handling them requires a different protocol than a standard rule-breaker.

  • Do not interact: Never speak to an exploiter. Never threaten them with a ban. They are seeking attention. Bringing attention to them in the chat gives them what they want.
  • Invisible Spectate: Use the ERLC spectate tools or fly high into the sky and use a sniper scope/freecam to record them. You MUST get a video clip of them exploiting before you ban them to protect yourself from false-ban appeals.
  • Immediate Permanent Ban: Do not warn. Do not kick. Issue a permanent server ban immediately using :ban [username] 0 Exploiting | [Your Name].
  • Discord Escalation: If the exploiter is crashing the server, instantly ping the Management team in the Discord emergency channel.
