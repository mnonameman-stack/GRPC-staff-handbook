Overview

Welcome to the GRPC Staff Team

As a staff member, you are the backbone of the community. Your primary goal is not just to punish rulebreakers, but to educate players, facilitate high-quality roleplay, and maintain a fair, immersive environment.

The Staff Mindset

Being a moderator requires patience, critical thinking, and a thick skin. Players in high-stress RP situations will often be angry or defensive when brought into a sit. Your job is to de-escalate the situation, review the facts objectively, and execute a fair judgment.

  • Educate Before You Eradicate: If a new player breaks a minor rule, explain *why* it's a rule before jumping to a ban.
  • Consistency is Key: Use the Punishment Matrix as your bible to ensure every player is treated equally.
  • Leave Bias at the Door: Your friends do not get special treatment. Period.

Procedures

Staff Conduct & Ticket Execution

How you handle a ticket determines the player's respect for the server. Professionalism is mandatory at all times.

Executing a Ticket (The Mod Sit)

1
Claim and Observe

When a ticket is submitted, claim it via the panel. Before teleporting, observe the logs or use noclip (while invisible) to watch the situation unfold if it is still active. Never disrupt an active RP scenario unless a catastrophic rulebreak is actively ruining it.

2
Isolate the Parties

Teleport the reporter and the reported player to a staff sit area (e.g., the roof of a tall building away from the public). Freeze them immediately if they are attempting to attack each other or run away.

3
Control the Comms

Players will often scream over each other. Use your authority to silence them. Say clearly: "Only one person speaks at a time. [Player A], you made the report, explain what happened." If Player B interrupts, warn them. If they continue, server-mute them temporarily.

4
Gather Evidence & Execute

Ask for video clips (Medal, Shadowplay). Logs show damage, but clips show *context*. Once evidence is reviewed, inform the offending player of the exact rule broken, issue the punishment via the panel, and return the victim to their previous RP scenario.

!
Staff Disrespect: You will encounter toxicity. Do not return the toxicity. If a player uses slurs or severe disrespect toward you, add a "Staff Disrespect" multiplier to their punishment and swiftly remove them.
Core Offenses

RDM & VDM Execution

Random Deathmatch (RDM) and Vehicle Deathmatch (VDM) are the most common and disruptive rulebreaks in any RP server. Understanding the nuances is critical.

Random Deathmatch (RDM)

Killing or attempting to kill another player without valid RP initiation or underlying RP motive.

What Counts as Valid Initiation?

  • A clear, audible vocal threat giving the player time to comply (e.g., "Put your hands up or I will shoot" - wait 3-5 seconds).
  • Active, ongoing gang wars where hostile intent has already been established globally.
  • Self-defense: If someone shoots at you first, you do not need to initiate to shoot back.

Handling RDM Claims

Always check the kill logs. If Player A claims RDM, check if Player A did damage to Player B earlier in the scene. Ask the shooter: "What was your RP reason for shooting?" If their answer is "He looked at me funny" or "He insulted my shoes," that is not a valid reason for murder. Issue the punishment.

Vehicle Deathmatch (VDM)

Using a vehicle as a weapon to intentionally run over, ram, or kill another player without a valid, extreme RP reason.

The "Desync" Defense

Players will frequently claim: "It was desync! He walked into the road on my screen!" How to investigate:

  1. Review the clip: Did the driver swerve *out of their lane* to hit the pedestrian? That's intentional.
  2. Speed & Braking: Did they brake at all? Did they speed up?
  3. Post-Impact: Did they stop to RP the accident (e.g., calling an ambulance), or did they reverse to run the body over again? The latter is blatant VDM.

Note on Fleeing: If a player is surrounded by armed robbers, using their car to push through the crowd to escape *is* allowed (FearRP/Self-Preservation). Hunting someone down with a car is not.

Core Offenses

Metagaming & Powergaming

These offenses destroy the integrity of the roleplay world by introducing outside information or impossible mechanics.

Metagaming

Using out-of-character (OOC) information to your in-character (IC) advantage.

  • Stream Sniping: Watching a police streamer to find out where they are setting up a speed trap.
  • Discord Calls: Being in a private Discord call with a friend and saying "Hey, I'm getting robbed at the bank, come shoot them!" instead of using the in-game radio.
  • Name Tags: Recognizing someone because you read their nametag through a wall or mask, not because you recognized their voice or outfit.

Powergaming (FailRP)

Forcing an RP situation on another player without giving them a chance to react, or doing things that are physically impossible in reality.

  • /me Abuse: Typing /me rips the gun out of his hands and knocks him out without giving the other player a chance to type /me attempts to resist.
  • Talking while dead/unconscious: Giving tactical callouts to your gang while lying dead on the ground.
  • Unrealistic driving: Ramping a sports car off a mountain, landing on the roof, flipping it over, and driving away as if nothing happened.
Core Offenses

FearRP & New Life Rule

FearRP (Value of Life)

Players must realistically fear for their lives when at a clear disadvantage.

Example Violation: A player has three guns pointed at their head, but they decide to pull out their own pistol and start shooting, or they just run away while being held at gunpoint. This is a severe breach of RP.

Mod Execution: Evaluate the timeline. Did the victim have a gun in their hand *before* the robbers aimed at them? If so, they can shoot back. If their weapon was holstered and 3 guns were drawn on them, they must comply. Punish for FailRP/No Fear.

New Life Rule (NLR)

If you die (are fully killed and respawn at the hospital, not just downed and revived by medics), you forget the events leading up to your death and cannot return to the scene of your death for 30 minutes.

Mod Execution: Often, players will get salty they died, respawn, grab a gun, and drive straight back to the shootout. Check the hospital respawn logs against the kill logs. If they return to the active scene, issue an immediate kick or ban. They are revenge-RDMing.

Enforcement

Complex Ticket Resolution

Not every ticket is black and white. Here is how you handle complex situations where players have conflicting stories and no video evidence.

The "He Said, She Said" Scenario

Player A claims Player B RDM'd them. Player B claims Player A pointed a gun at them first. Neither has a clip.

⚖️
The Golden Rule of Evidence: No Clip = No Action (usually). We do not ban based purely on word-of-mouth.

Investigation Steps:

  1. Check Weapons Logs: Did Player A even have a gun in their inventory to point at Player B? If not, Player B is lying.
  2. Check Chat/Voice Logs (if applicable): Was there any initiation registered in proximity?
  3. Look for Witnesses: Were there neutral third parties around? Ask them.
  4. The Resolution: If you truly cannot determine who is at fault, you must void the RP scenario. Revive the dead player, heal them, return their lost items, and issue a strict verbal warning to both parties to run recording software (like Medal) in the future.

Handling Exploiting/Hacking Accusations

If someone is accused of using an aimbot or economy exploit, do not confront them immediately. Turn on your invisibility, spectate them silently, and record them yourself. Once you have undeniable proof, ban them permanently and notify the Admin team to wipe their account assets.
