Module 01 · Preparations

Pre-Operation Setup

Everything before the first phase: posting the notice, holding the room, and setting expectations. Get this right and the session runs itself.

MDT-01
Timing & Capture
Training Window
OPEN · 10 MIN
+3 GRACE
0 min10 min — close13 max

Hold the room open for 10 minutes. You may extend to a hard cap of 13 minutes — the 3-minute grace is only for trainees running late.

Run Order
Post the notice in #training-notice — build it in the Dispatch module.
Open the room and start the 10-minute timer; answer attendee questions while you wait.
Once everyone is seated, take the wide starting screenshot — every trainee visible.
Lock the room and move to the opening Briefing.
Conduct & Discretion
  • A trainee who misbehaves or breaks the rules may be warned, struck, or removed at the host's discretion.
  • SPaG is enforced at all times. A trainee gets 2 warnings before strikes begin — this rule is mandatory.
  • Strikes carry across the whole session. Whenever a trainee earns one, tell them their current total.
  • If miscommunication results from the trainer's inattention, the trainer may be warned.
Group AdaptationsMULTIPLE TRAINEES

With more than one trainee, four phases change. Single-trainee sessions follow the standard handbook flow.

Team Leader selection. When grouping trainees, base the Team Leader choice strictly on demonstrated SPaG and the lowest warning count — not on who speaks first.

Module 04 · Reference

Knowledge Base

The group quiz, radio-code reference, and terminology. Reveal the evaluation guidance under each quiz question — use it to judge answers, not to read aloud.

MDT-04
Quiz ProtocolGROUP SESSIONS
  • Trainees ping you in the trainee chat; you send the quiz and they have 5 minutes to answer — all answers in SPaG.
  • Scoring: a wrong answer is −1; an answer missing SPaG is −0.5. A trainee must score 2 of 3 correct.
  • Send results once everyone is done. Below 2 correct earns a strike — inform the trainee. The quiz is changeable.
1You are running Code 3 (lights and sirens) and turning onto a road where another officer is also running Code 3 and will crash into you if you turn. You are on an emergency and on-duty as a whitelisted officer. Do you still turn onto that road — and why?
Acceptable answer demonstrates

The trainee should yield and avoid the collision — you do not turn into the other unit's path. Right-of-way during an emergency is never a licence to cause a crash; colliding ends both responses and endangers everyone. Look for an answer that puts collision avoidance above speed.

2The President is about to be swarmed by multiple armed civilians at the White House, but you are currently in a Mod Scene. Do you assist the President or hold — and why?
Acceptable answer demonstrates

The trainee should hold and stay frozen. A Mod Scene pauses all roleplay; acting during one is fail-roleplay regardless of what is happening in-character. The correct call is to wait for the moderator to lift the scene, then respond.

3Can a Washington Metropolitan Police Department officer protect the President — why or why not?
Acceptable answer demonstrates

Judge this against your community's canon. A strong answer recognises that MPD's role is general law enforcement and support — officers may assist and secure an area — while dedicated executive protection is the protective detail's responsibility, not a substitute MPD provides. Reward clear reasoning over a one-word reply.
