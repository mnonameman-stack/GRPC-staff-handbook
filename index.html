Orientation

Staff Expectations

What GRPC expects from every staff member every single shift — not occasionally, but every time you go on duty.

Professionalism

You represent GRPC the moment you put on your staff tag. Players form opinions about the entire community based on how you act. At all times remain calm, neutral, and professional — even when a player is being abusive or testing your patience. Do not argue in public chat. Do not get emotional. Handle situations via PM or modcalls, and let your actions speak rather than your words.

iA staff member who stays calm under pressure earns far more respect than one who claps back at rule-breakers. The community is watching.

Activity & Quotas

Every rank has a weekly activity quota tracked via Melonly. Failing to meet quota without an approved Leave of Absence (LOA) will result in a strike or demotion. Quota hours must be completed while actively on duty — AFK time does not count. LOA requests must be submitted before your quota period ends, not after.

Communication

Read all staff announcements within 48 hours of posting. Use proper spelling and grammar (SPaG) in all communications — public chat, announcements, PMs, and Discord. Shorthand and slang are unprofessional. If you wouldn't say it in front of Leadership, don't say it at all.

Bias & Fairness

Moderate fairly regardless of who the player is. Treating friends more leniently — or being harsher toward people you dislike — is bias moderation. If you have a personal relationship with someone involved in an incident, escalate it to another staff member. Bias moderation leads to demotion or removal.

Behaviour Off Duty

You are still a GRPC staff member when not on shift. Representing the community negatively on other servers or in public chats reflects on GRPC and may result in disciplinary action. Never badmouth GRPC, its leadership, or fellow staff members publicly.

Escalation Judgement

One of the most important skills you will develop is knowing when to handle something yourself and when to escalate. As a rough guide: if you are confident in the punishment and it falls clearly within the guide, handle it. If it involves a grey area, a senior staff member, a potential permanent ban, or an internal conflict — escalate. A wrong action is always worse than a delayed correct one.

Roleplay Operations

Roleplay Overview

Understanding what roleplay is, why GRPC runs it, and the universal principles that govern every session.

What is Roleplay?

Roleplay (RP) is the core activity of GRPC. Players take on fictional roles — president, soldier, law enforcement, civilian — and act within those roles to create an immersive, story-driven experience. Staff have two jobs during any RP session: facilitate (keep it moving, make announcements, set up environments) and enforce (stop rule-breakers from ruining the experience for everyone else).

Staff Role During Roleplay

When an RP session is active, the hosting staff member is the Scene Commander. All moderation decisions during that session should be routed through or communicated to the host. Other on-shift staff act as support — handling modcalls, patrolling for VDM/RDM, and maintaining order outside the main RP zone.

Universal RP Rules

  • Every RP must be announced at the start using the correct announcement script from the relevant section of this handbook.
  • Players must stay within the designated RP zone unless directed otherwise by staff.
  • Safe zones are always off-limits to RP violence — no exceptions, ever.
  • Staff must update the server on the state of the RP every 30–60 seconds during active sessions.
  • No ELS, honking, or reckless driving in convoy unless you are LEO actively pursuing a criminal in-RP.
  • Exploits, hacks, and glitches during RP result in an immediate ban regardless of prior history.
  • Non-RP players who enter an active RP zone and disrupt it after one warning are removed immediately — no second warning during active RP.

Starting Any Roleplay Session

1
Confirm rank requirements

Most RPs require Supervisor (SV) or above to load a layout. Check the specific RP page before proceeding.

2
Load the layout

Use :loadlayout [name]. Confirm it loaded before announcing. If it fails twice, report to a higher rank and fall back to Normal RP.

3
Make the server-wide announcement

Use :m for critical messages (red) and :h for regular updates (blue). Use the scripts from the relevant RP section verbatim — consistency matters.

4
Assign support staff and begin active patrol

Don't stand still. Move around the RP area, monitor all chats, and respond to modcalls promptly. A stationary moderator misses half of what is happening.

Moderation Rules

To be a good moderator you need to follow the rules below. Not following them may — and will — result in punishment.

  • Use proper spelling and grammar (SPaG) at all times.
  • Only Senior High Rank (SHR) and above may use commands while off duty.
  • Do not allow people to break the rules in front of you.
  • Do not shift farm or abuse your powers.
  • Follow the Chain of Command.
  • Do not disrespect anyone.
  • Using commands while in the Leadership server (LS) is prohibited.
  • Do not ask for a promotion.
  • Enforce the rules strictly and follow direction from your High Ranks.
iWhen in doubt, follow the Chain of Command — escalate to the next rank above yours rather than guessing.

President Roleplay

Our most common roleplay. Run it with a High Rank or above using the loadlayout command for the presidential layouts.

1
Call the election — announce, wait, announce twice more
:m The presidential elections are happening at the Postal office rooftop. Come and join to possibly rule the country of America!
wait ~60 seconds
:h The presidential elections are happening at the Postal office rooftop. Come and join to possibly rule the country of America!
wait ~60 seconds
:h The presidential elections are happening at the Postal office rooftop. Come and join to possibly rule the country of America!
2
Once 4+ benches are full, open the election
:h Thank you everyone for attending the elections! I will now be closing three candidates who will run for the president.

Choose the candidates yourself, then announce:

:h The candidates have been chosen! They will now be given a minute to give their speeches, after which you may vote.
wait ~60 seconds
:h Voting begins! Vote for the person you'd like to see as the president!
wait ~60 seconds
3
Announce the winners
:m The president has been chosen as [username] followed by [username] as his VP and [username] as the Secretary of defense! Please prepare a proper convoy behind the staff vehicle so we may begin.

Replace every bracketed name with the actual usernames elected.

4
Escort the president to the Hospital ("the Whitehouse")

Watch the convoy closely for VDM, shooting, or other disturbances — warn, load, or jail anyone abusing it. Once he arrives, set a 60-second PRTY and announce:

:h The president [username] has arrived to [location]! All LEO are requested to protect him, criminals may begin their attacks after the timer has ended.
5
Keep the server updated every 30s – 1 min
:h The president is [user] and he is at the [location] with [lives] while currently being [situation]. Please come and join the roleplay to have fun!
6
If the president runs out of lives

The president has 3 lives. If he loses all of them, the VP gets one chance to escape to the limousine before he dies once. If he makes it, he becomes president. If he dies on the way, a new election starts. Announce the escape:

:h The VP is making an emergency escape to the limousine! If he makes it to it, he will take over the role of the President. All LEO MUST protect him on the route there and criminals may try and attack him to remove him from the office.
iThe president may roam anywhere on the map except safe zones — that's a hard no, and it usually triggers shootings. During the convoy, no one may use ELS, honk, overtake, or ram the staff vehicle.

Normal Roleplay

We try to avoid this one, but sometimes it's necessary.

Normal roleplay has no mod-set scenario — people do whatever they want. Use it when player count is low, staff is thin, or there's another reasonable cause. It's not the recommended option, so reach for it last.

Roleplay Operations

Military War Roleplay BETA

RC and SF go to war. Still in beta — expect changes.

1. Elect two leaders

Run the same election announcements as President Roleplay. Once the winners are announced, the Vice President revolts and starts his own nation in SF, igniting the war:

:m ATTENTION! The Vice-president has started a revolution against the president. A war between SF and RC has broke out. All people are required to pick a team and serve in their military.

2. Set up the war

  • A co-owner must turn on team killing.
  • Each leader (SF and RC) starts with 3 lives.
  • Every player must join either the WL or Police department, in their team's uniform and livery.
  • RC wears green camo; SF wears yellow/sand camo.
  • The team whose president still has lives left when the other hits zero wins, and normal President Roleplay resumes.

3. Hosting

You need two hosts — one stays with the RC president, one stays with the SF president — plus a supervisor acting as main host who keeps the server updated:

:h A war is currently ongoing between SF and RC. All people MUST be on the Police teams, equip their respective team uniform, and engage in combat.
:h There is a war going on in SF/RC. The RC president [user] has [lives] and the SF president [user] has [lives].

Repeat the status update roughly every minute.

Each host must report their president's deaths and start a 60-second PT immediately after:

The president of [RC/SF] has lost a life. He has [number of lives] left. The opposing team may attack after the PT is over.
iKeep staff patrolling the war zones, and post one staff member at the Police station so the opposing team can't shoot inside it.

In-Game Command Usage

Minimum characters

Always use at least four letters of a username when targeting someone with a command. Going shorter risks hitting the wrong person.

!Example: trying to heal "jarvis" with :heal ja could accidentally heal "jalin" instead.

Off-duty commands

Only Senior High Rank (SHR) and above may use commands while off duty, or stay on as a civilian / in roleplay while on duty. Anyone else doing this counts as shift farming and will be struck.

All / Others / M commands

These are off-limits unless a Supervisor or above approves them first. If someone with "All" or "Others" in their username is trolling, ban-bolo them — that's an attempt to trigger mass chaos or a soft raid.

Peacetimer

350 is the hard cap. Going over it gets you infracted regardless of rank, unless there's mass RDM or it's been approved by an SHR+.

Enforcement

Fast-Paced Regulations

Logging roleplay

Hostage situations, kidnappings, and DOT roadblock scenes must be logged in #roleplay-logs before they happen — otherwise the scene needs to be voided, or you need direct permission to continue it.

Weapons & force

Staff may only use the following to stop a suspect:

LasersPepper sprayBeanbag shotgunStop sticks:down command

If the suspect is still running after that, jail them and PM their punishment.

Pursuits

Only chase when you can't see the person's username and you directly witness a rule violation — or you have valid proof of one. You can grapple someone who won't pull over. Not pulling over earns a warning; running after that counts as staff evasion.

Evidence

Only screenshots and clips count as valid evidence. Kill logs and witness reports don't count unless an on-duty staff member personally witnessed it.

Chain of Command

Follow the CoC at all times to avoid punishment — if you need help, go to the next rank above yours.

Bands narrow toward the top: fewer seats, more responsibility. Each card shows the rank cap and the in-game quota.

iHelpers sit alongside this chain. They aren't technically moderators, but they do hold admin permissions — they're trusted and closely supervised so those permissions don't get abused.

Operations Tools

Melonly Logging

Melonly is mandatory every time you go on duty.

  • Log every shift, punishment, and related action through Melonly.
  • High Ranks must both accept a ban-bolo in Melonly and ban the user in-game to confirm the ban actually took.
  • Any unban must be logged by a High Rank.
!Not using Melonly leads to harsh punishment — there's no exception for forgetting.

Operations Tools

Staff Hangout

A break space — not a place to moderate from.

  • Reserved for staff who are on break. If you're actively moderating or handling duties, don't linger here unless you're authorized to.
  • Official meetings and briefings can be held here — stay professional and respectful throughout.
  • Staff events and team-building activities are welcome, as long as everyone follows server rules and staff conduct policy.
