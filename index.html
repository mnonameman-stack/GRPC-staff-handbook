Orientation

Government Roleplay Community

Official GRPC Staff Guide v2.0 — the complete operating manual for every rank, from Trial Moderator to Chairman.

!This guide is for GRPC staff only. Leaking any part of it outside the staff team will lead to an SBL or blacklist.

GRPC exists to deliver a structured, immersive government-and-military roleplay experience built on top of ERLC. Unlike a standard ERLC police/fire server, GRPC layers a presidential government simulation, organized military operations, and large-scale civil events on top of the base game. This handbook covers every core thing you need to know — moderation rules, how to host each roleplay type, the punishment guide, chain of command, and day-to-day operating procedures.

This handbook is not a casual FAQ. It is the document HR, IA, and Directory will reference when reviewing your conduct, evaluating you for promotion, or investigating a complaint. You are expected to know it well enough that you do not need to ask a senior staff member basic procedural questions in staff chat.

Every rank from Trial Moderator through Chairman is bound by this handbook without exception. Higher ranks are trusted with more powerful commands and held to a stricter standard of conduct — not a looser one.

Orientation

Moderation Rules

These rules are the baseline standard of conduct every staff member must follow on every shift, regardless of rank. Violating them is treated the same as a player violating the Punishment Guide — except staff are held to a higher standard.

  • Use proper spelling and grammar (SPaG) at all times. Every message you send on duty — in-game, in announcements, or in Melonly logs — reflects GRPC's professionalism. Use full sentences, correct capitalization, and proper punctuation, especially in scripted :h and :m announcements.
  • Only Senior High Rank (SHR) and above may use commands while off duty. If you are below SHR and witness rule-breaking while off duty, screen-record evidence and report it to an on-duty staff member — do not mod off duty.
  • Do not allow people to break the rules in front of you. Turning a blind eye to violations — because the offender is a friend or you "don't feel like dealing with it" — is itself a punishable offense.
  • Do not shift farm or abuse your powers. Shift farming means starting a Melonly shift and going AFK or playing as a civilian simply to accumulate quota hours. Power abuse means using commands for personal benefit rather than legitimate moderation.
  • Follow the Chain of Command. Escalate issues to the next rank above yours rather than acting outside your authority or arguing directives live in front of players.
  • Do not disrespect anyone. This includes players, fellow staff, and HR. Sarcasm, condescension, or "ratioing" a player while moderating them is still disrespect, even if the player broke a rule first. De-escalate, don't retaliate.
  • Using commands on LS (Low Severity) cases is prohibited. Reserve command usage for legitimate, Punishment-Guide-backed violations — not minor, unintentional, first-time incidents.
  • Do not ask for a promotion. Promotions are evaluated by HR and Directory based on logged activity and professionalism. Repeatedly asking for a promotion is viewed negatively and can actually delay your review cycle.
  • Enforce the rules strictly and follow your HR's direction. Strict enforcement means applying the Punishment Guide consistently, without bending thresholds for friends or out of intimidation toward higher ranks.
iWhen in doubt, follow the Chain of Command — escalate to the next rank above yours rather than guessing. Every one of these rules maps to a real failure mode that has historically damaged ERLC communities.

Roleplay Operations

Border Roleplay

One of the simpler, lower-prep events GRPC hosts — but simple does not mean low effort. It is often the first event a Trial Moderator or Supervisor will be trusted to host.

Border Roleplay takes place at the HRP (High Rock Park) border crossing and simulates a national border checkpoint. Players organize their own scenes — customs checks, smuggling attempts, vehicle searches, standoffs — without a rigid mod-assigned script. This makes host presence and rule enforcement especially important.

Requirements

You must be at least SV+ (Supervisor or above) to load the Border layout. The loadlayout command can disrupt active roleplay if misused, and Border events draw larger, less predictable crowds.

Hosting Procedure

1
Load the layout as SV+

Double-check the layout has fully loaded — props that spawn incorrectly (overlapping barriers, misplaced gates) will cause confusion and exploit opportunities.

2
Announce the event
:h Welcome to GRPC! The current roleplay is Border at the HRP border. Please come to roleplay and hope you have a great time!
3
Position yourself and monitor

Stand at or near the checkpoint with sightlines down both sides of the border line. Let roleplay develop organically — your job is to watch for violations, not to direct the scene. Re-announce every 5–10 minutes of low activity to draw new players in.

Common Border-Specific Violations

  • Vehicles ramming the gate (VDM / GTAD).
  • Players skipping the queue by going off-road around the checkpoint (FRP / exploiting terrain).
  • Weapons drawn without an escalating, realistic reason (Fear RP violations).
!Border Roleplay has no formal win condition. End it using your discretion when player count drops significantly or after a reasonable runtime (typically 20–40 minutes). There is no need for a formal "event over" announcement.

Roleplay Operations

President Roleplay

GRPC's flagship and most frequently hosted event. A multi-phase, scripted government simulation culminating in an election, a convoy, and an ongoing president-protection gameplay loop.

A High Rank+ must run the loadlayout command to spawn all presidential event props. Lower ranks may assist with announcements and crowd control once the layout is active, but cannot independently initiate the event.

1
Call the election — three staged announcements

Send all three in sequence, spaced roughly 60 seconds apart. Do not send them back-to-back — the timing gives players across the map time to travel to the Postal Office.

:m The presidential elections are happening at the Postal office rooftop. Come and join to possibly rule the country of America!
wait ~60 seconds
:h The presidential elections are happening at the Postal office rooftop. Come and join to possibly rule the country of America!
wait ~60 seconds
:h The presidential elections are happening at the Postal office rooftop. Come and join to possibly rule the country of America!
2
Confirm quorum (4+ full benches), then open the election

If turnout is low after the third announcement, wait 1–2 more minutes before continuing. Rotate candidate selection — avoid picking the same handful of regulars every election.

:h Thank you everyone for attending the elections! I will now be closing three candidates who will run for the president.

Select three candidates, then announce:

:h The candidates have been chosen! They will now be given a minute to give their speeches, after which you may vote.
wait ~60 seconds for speeches
:h Voting begins! Vote for the person you'd like to see as the president!
wait ~60 seconds for voting
3
Announce the winners
:m The president has been chosen as [username] followed by [username] as his VP and [username] as the Secretary of defense! Please prepare a proper convoy behind the staff vehicle so we may begin.

Replace every bracketed name with the actual elected usernames before sending. Sending the template with brackets still showing is unprofessional and confuses players.

4
Escort the president to the Hospital ("the Whitehouse") — the convoy

Watch constantly for VDM, unauthorized shooting, and general disruption. During the convoy, no player may use ELS, honk, overtake, or ram the staff vehicle — this applies to escorting staff and civilian onlookers alike. Assign a dedicated staff member to actively watch the convoy route for VDM. Once the President arrives, set a 60-second PRTY and announce:

:h The president [username] has arrived to [location]! All LEO are requested to protect him, criminals may begin their attacks after the timer has ended.
5
Keep the server updated every 30s – 1 min

Treat this as a recurring task throughout the remainder of the event, not a one-time announcement. Keep location/lives/situation fields accurate each time.

:h The president is [user] and he is at the [location] with [lives] while currently being [situation]. Please come and join the roleplay to have fun!
6
VP succession — if the president runs out of lives

The president has 3 lives. Once all three are lost, the VP gets one chance to escape to the limousine. If the VP dies during the escape, a brand-new election begins from Phase 1. If the VP reaches the limo safely, he becomes the new President with 3 lives.

:h The VP is making an emergency escape to the limousine! If he makes it to it, he will take over the role of the President. All LEO MUST protect him on the route there and criminals may try and attack him to remove him from the office.
!The president may roam anywhere on the map except designated Safe Zones — entering a Safe Zone creates an unenforceable shooting situation inside a no-combat area. During the convoy, no one may use ELS, honk, overtake, or ram the staff vehicle.

Roleplay Operations

Military Roleplay

Hosted across all of Springfield and High Rock Park — the largest operating area of any GRPC event type. Can be player-built or mod-assigned.

Format Options

  • Player-built scenarios — squads organize their own war games, patrols, or training exercises with no top-down mod direction.
  • Mod-assigned scenarios — a host designs and assigns a specific scenario (e.g., "Team A holds the ridge, Team B attempts to retake it").
  • War scenarios — larger-scale faction-vs-faction combat roleplay.
  • Border-style military presence — units stationed at or patrolling the border.
  • Park ranger / patrol scenarios — lower-intensity roleplay focused on patrol and presence rather than combat.

The defining requirement across every variation: no rules being broken, and everyone complying with staff direction.

Announcement

:h The current roleplay is Military at Springfield/ High rock. Come and join to have a nice time and enjoy your stay here at GRPC!

Hosting Responsibilities

  • Position yourself centrally or rotate your patrol route to cover both Springfield and High Rock Park — do not camp in one corner of the large operating area.
  • Watch closely for FRP during combat — players "tanking" unrealistic damage, ignoring being downed, or refusing to roleplay injuries.
  • Watch for NLR violations — players returning to the same fight minutes after dying.
  • Keep weapon usage within server rules. The banned weapons list still applies unless explicitly authorized for the scenario.
  • If you assigned a mod-preset scenario, periodically check that both sides are still complying rather than devolving into unstructured chaos.
iMilitary Roleplay has no fixed win condition unless the scenario you assigned includes one. Use your judgment to wind the event down once engagement drops or a natural narrative conclusion is reached.

Roleplay Operations

Normal Roleplay

GRPC's lowest-structure event type. Treated as a fallback rather than a primary offering — reach for it last.

During Normal Roleplay there is no mod preset, no scripted announcements, and no assigned scenario. Players are free to roleplay civilian life, run their own small scenes, or simply enjoy the server casually.

When to use it

  • Player count is too low to realistically support a structured event like President or Military Roleplay.
  • Staff count is too thin to safely host and monitor a larger scripted event.
  • There is another documented reason a structured event isn't feasible (server lag, a recent disruptive incident that needs to settle, etc.).

Staff responsibilities

Even without a scripted event to run, moderation duties do not relax. Normal Roleplay periods require more individual vigilance since there's no central event drawing your attention to one location — violations can happen anywhere simultaneously. Actively patrol, enforce the full Punishment Guide, and use the opportunity to handle backlog tasks without neglecting active moderation.

!Normal Roleplay does not showcase GRPC's unique identity. New and returning players who join during a Normal Roleplay period may not understand what makes GRPC different. Whenever player and staff count support it, prioritize a structured event instead.

Roleplay Operations

Military War Roleplay BETA

GRPC's most ambitious event. Begins as President Roleplay, then the VP revolts — triggering a two-faction war between RC and SF. Still in active beta; procedures will evolve.

1. Run the election (identical to President Roleplay)

Follow the exact same announcement and election sequence as standard President Roleplay. Confirm 4+ full benches, close candidates, run speeches, and hold the vote.

:m The presidential elections are happening at the Postal office rooftop. Come and join to possibly rule the country of America!

2. Announce results — then the twist

:m The president has been chosen as [username] followed by [username] as his VP and [username] as the Secretary of defense! Please prepare a proper convoy behind the staff vehicle so we may begin.

Immediately follow with the VP revolt announcement:

:m ATTENTION! The Vice-president has started a revolution against the president. A war between SF and RC has broke out. All people are required to pick a team and serve in their military.

3. Set up the war

  • A Co-Owner must turn on team killing for the duration. This toggle should never be left on outside of an active, supervised MwR event.
  • Each faction leader (RC President and SF VP-turned-leader) has 3 lives. The first leader to lose all 3 loses the war.
  • Every player must join either the WL or Police department in their team's uniform and livery.
  • RC wears green camo; SF wears sandy/yellow-ish desert camouflage. Staff must be able to identify a player's faction by livery alone without asking.

4. Staffing requirements

MwR requires at least three coordinated staff members:

  • RC Host — stays with the RC President at all times, tracking lives and protecting against unfair RDM.
  • SF Host — stays with the SF leader at all times, performing the same function for the opposing faction.
  • Supervisor / Main Host — does not attach to either leader. Acts as the central announcer and overall event coordinator.

5. Ongoing announcements

The Supervisor/Main Host sends recurring status updates roughly every minute:

:h A war is currently ongoing between SF and RC. All people MUST be on the Police teams, equipt their respectable team uniform and engage in combat.
:h There is a war going on in SF/RC. The RC president [user] has [lives] and the SF president [user] has [lives].

Repeat the status update roughly every minute.

Each faction Host reports their leader's deaths and starts a 60-second Protection Timer immediately after:

The president of [RC/SF] Has lost a life. He has [number of lives] left. The oppositioning team may attack after the PT is over.
iKeep staff patrolling the active war zones to catch RDM, VDM, and FRP in the wider battle — not just incidents involving the leadership. Post one staff member at the Police Station specifically to prevent combat inside it. MwR is still in beta; always check for the latest handbook version before hosting.

Enforcement

Punishment Guide

Two staff members handling the same offense, committed by two different players, should reach the same outcome. Inconsistent enforcement is one of the fastest ways to lose a community's trust.

Every offense escalates across three tiers — 1st time, 2nd time, and 3rd time. "Time" refers to that specific offense type for that specific player, tracked via their Melonly punishment history. Always check Melonly logs before issuing any punishment.

!Any offense committed inside a Safe Zone receives an automatic escalation: add 30 minutes to any kick-tier punishment, add 1 day to any ban-tier punishment, and any offense that would normally only warrant a warning is upgraded to a 30-minute kick.

Offense Definitions

RDM (Random Deathmatch)Killing or attacking a player without a valid, realistic, in-character reason. One of the most common reports — almost always reported with screenshot or clip evidence showing an unprovoked kill.
VDM (Vehicle Deathmatch)Using a vehicle as a weapon to intentionally hit, ram, or kill another player without realistic justification. A genuine accidental fender-bender roleplayed out is not VDM; deliberately swerving into pedestrians is.
FRP / NITRP (Fail Roleplay / No Intent to Roleplay)FRP covers any action that breaks realism or immersion. NITRP is the narrower case where a player demonstrates they have no interest in engaging with roleplay at all — spawn-killing, trolling builds, or openly stating they're "just here to ruin it."
Fear RPPlayers must roleplay realistic fear when their life is credibly threatened (a weapon at close range, being surrounded). Continuing to fight back or run from an active, realistic threat is a Fear RP violation.
GTAD (GTA Driving)Reckless, unrealistic driving that ignores traffic laws — flying through intersections at unrealistic speed, driving on sidewalks without RP justification, or weaving through traffic with no regard for collisions.
NLR (New Life Rule)Once a character dies or is arrested, that "life" is over. Returning to the same scene to seek revenge or share information your dead character shouldn't have is a New Life Rule violation.
Tool abuseMisusing build tools, admin tools, or in-game mechanics outside their intended purpose — spawning props to grief, block roads maliciously, or create unfair advantages.
Staff evasionDeliberately avoiding, ignoring, or running from a staff member who is actively attempting to address you regarding a possible violation.
SRDM (Staff RDM)Committing RDM specifically against an on-duty staff member. Treated as its own category because attacking staff disrupts the entire moderation process for every other player — not just the staff member targeted.
LTAA (Leaving to Avoid Arrest)Disconnecting specifically to avoid being arrested or detained mid-scene by Law Enforcement roleplay, separate from avoiding a staff punishment (see LTAP).
CR and AJ (Cuff Rushing / Auto Jailing)Cuff Rushing is restraining a player without roleplaying the arrest sequence. Auto Jailing is sending a player straight to jail without realistically transporting them there in-game first. Both break immersion and are treated as seriously as player-side FRP.
ExploitingUsing bugs, glitches, or external scripts/cheats to gain an unfair advantage. One of the most severe offenses — 100-day ban on a first offense, escalating to permanent — because exploits can compromise the entire server.
RacismAny racist remarks, slurs, or discriminatory content. Carries one of the longest minimum ban durations and is never tolerated regardless of "joking" intent.
NSFWSharing or producing not-safe-for-work content through any GRPC-affiliated platform.
Rejoin before timerReconnecting to the server before a kick or ban duration has expired, which Melonly and/or Roblox moderation tools should flag automatically.
BypassingCircumventing the chat filter through alternate spellings, spacing, or special characters.
LTAP (Leaving to Avoid Punishment)Disconnecting specifically to dodge an active staff investigation or pending punishment. One of the more serious offenses because it actively obstructs the moderation process.
TOS (Terms of Service violation)Any action that violates Roblox's platform-wide Terms of Service, which GRPC is obligated to enforce regardless of internal rules.
Staff impersonationFalsely claiming to be a GRPC staff member, wearing unauthorized staff-branded items, or attempting to issue "fake" moderation actions while not actually on duty.
TrollingGeneral disruptive behavior not specifically covered by another category — intentionally annoying players, spamming low-effort scenes, or degrading the roleplay experience without rising to the level of a more specific offense.

Safe Zones & Restrictions

Safe Zones exist to give players a guaranteed area free from combat, vehicle violence, and roleplay-related chaos. Enforcing them consistently is one of the highest-leverage things you can do as a moderator.

Designated Safe Zones

What is prohibited in Safe Zones

No combat, weapon usage, vehicle ramming, or aggressive roleplay of any kind is permitted within these areas. This includes drawing a weapon "for show," GTAD/reckless driving through the zone, and "zone camping" — using the boundary to ambush players the moment they exit (treated as FRP since it weaponizes the safe zone against the players it's meant to protect).

!The presidential Safe Zone restriction also applies during MwR. The elected President (and both faction leaders during MwR) is strictly barred from entering Safe Zones at any point during an active event — doing so creates an impossible enforcement situation where attackers are incentivized to break rules.

Banned vehicles

Any vehicle priced over 170,000 cash is banned by default. Unlock methods: active Booster status, active Membership, or purchasing the role through the server's economy. Verify status via Melonly or Discord roles before taking action — false positives here are a common source of player frustration.

Enforcement

In-Game Command Usage

Commands are powerful, and misusing them — even accidentally — can ruin an innocent player's experience or open the door to abuse allegations.

Minimum character requirement

When targeting a player by username, you must use at least four letters of their username. Roblox usernames frequently overlap in their first few characters, and a short partial match can easily catch the wrong person.

!Example: trying to heal "jarvis" with :heal ja could accidentally heal "jalin" instead. Always use at least :heal jarv (four characters minimum) before executing, and confirm the full username in the player list where possible.

Off-duty command restrictions

Only SHR+ (Senior High Rank and above) may use moderation commands while off duty, or remain on as a civilian without being actively clocked into a Melonly shift. Any staff member below SHR found using commands off-shift will be flagged for shift farming and struck accordingly.

All / Others / M command restrictions

Server-wide commands targeting all players, "others," or using :m broadcast are strictly prohibited unless explicitly approved by an SV+. These have server-wide blast radius — a mistake here doesn't affect one player, it affects everyone online.

If a player has crafted a username containing "all" or "others" to bait staff into accidentally mass-targeting the server, you are authorized to Ban BOLO them for attempting a soft raid via command exploitation — even if this technically involves an "all/others"-adjacent naming pattern. This exception covers deliberate exploitation, not an innocent coincidence.

Peace timer limits

The maximum peace timer is 350 seconds. Setting a peace timer higher than this — regardless of rank — will be flagged as an infraction, unless justified by mass RDM and approved by SHR+. Abusing an inflated PT to effectively freeze gameplay frustrates the broader player base.

Enforcement

Fast-Paced Regulations

Certain categories of high-stakes roleplay carry a high risk of spiraling into unmanageable chaos if left unchecked. This section governs those specific fast-paced scenarios.

Logging roleplay

Hostage situations, kidnappings, and DOT roadblock scenes must be logged in #roleplay-logs before or as they begin. An unlogged high-intensity scene looks — from a moderation standpoint — indistinguishable from an actual RDM spree in progress.

If a scene is not logged: the person running it must void it immediately, or obtain explicit permission from an on-duty staff member to continue retroactively.

Weapons & force — staff-side restrictions

When physically stopping a suspect (as opposed to using moderation commands), you are restricted to:

LasersPepper sprayBeanbag shotgunsStop sticks:down command

If the suspect is still running after these tools have been used, jail them directly and PM them their punishment afterward.

Pursuits

Vehicle or foot pursuits are only authorized when (1) you cannot identify the player's username through normal means, and (2) you directly witness a clear rule violation or have valid evidence of one. During an authorized pursuit you may use a grapple to stop a fleeing vehicle. Failure to pull over is itself punishable — starting with a warning, escalating to a Staff Evasion charge if they continue to flee.

Evidence standards

  • Only screenshots or video clips count as valid evidence for punishment decisions.
  • Kill logs alone do not count — a kill log shows that something happened, but not the surrounding context needed to judge whether it was a violation.
  • Witness testimony alone does not count, unless the witness is a staff member who was actively on duty and observed the incident directly.
iIf you only have a verbal report with no clip and no on-duty staff witness, you do not yet have grounds to punish — gather better evidence or monitor the situation directly instead.

Structure

Chain of Command

Follow the CoC at all times. If you need help, go to the next rank above yours — not several tiers at once unless the situation is severe.

Bands narrow toward the top: fewer seats, more responsibility. Each card shows the rank cap and in-game quota. IA operates as a parallel track rather than a simple continuation of the Moderator/Admin ladder.

iHelpers sit outside the formal CoC ladder. They are not technically "staff" in the traditional ranked sense, but they hold admin-level in-game permissions and are extremely closely supervised. Any sign of command misuse is escalated to Internal Affairs immediately.

Escalation Principles

  • Always escalate to the next tier up, not several tiers at once, unless the situation requires immediate Directory or IA involvement (active exploiting, staff power abuse, handbook leaks).
  • HR (High Rank) is the default escalation point for layout loading, all/others/m approval, or any judgment call above Supervisor authority.
  • Internal Affairs is the correct escalation for staff conduct issues specifically — not general player disputes, which stay within the standard Moderator → Admin → Supervisor → HR chain.

Structure

Promotions & Activity

Promotions are earned through demonstrated activity, professionalism, and event-hosting quality — never through simply asking, tenure alone, or social connections.

Eligibility

To be considered eligible for a promotion you must hold a low rank (anywhere from Moderator through Admin tier) and have logged at least 5 hours of shift time in Melonly within the relevant evaluation period. The 5-hour threshold is a floor, not a guarantee — HR and Management also weigh the quality of your logged hours.

What reviewers actually look at

  • Consistency — regular, predictable activity over the evaluation period, not a single burst of hours right before a review cycle.
  • Event hosting quality — how well you execute the scripted announcements and procedures in this handbook.
  • Punishment Guide accuracy — are your punishments correctly tiered based on player Melonly history?
  • Professionalism and SPaG — consistent with the Moderation Rules, your in-game and Melonly-logged communication should read as professional.
  • Absence of recent infractions — a recent strike, warning, or IA case will typically delay your next eligible review cycle.

Operations Tools

Melonly Logging

Melonly is GRPC's official moderation logging platform, and using it correctly is mandatory — not optional — for every shift you take. Without consistent logging, the Punishment Guide's escalation system cannot function.

Starting and ending shifts

  • Use Start Shift the moment you begin actively moderating — not after you've already started taking actions in-game.
  • Use the Break function for short pauses (5–10 minutes) rather than ending and restarting your shift.
  • End Shift the moment you stop actively moderating. Leaving a shift running while AFK or playing as a civilian is shift farming — Melonly's dashboard makes this pattern easy for HR to spot.
  • Use View My Shifts periodically to catch any logging mistakes early. This is the same data HR references during promotion and quota reviews.

Logging punishments

Every punishment — warnings, kicks, jails, and bans — must be logged in Melonly immediately after you issue it, not at the end of your shift. Delayed logging is one of the most common causes of incorrect escalation. Include enough detail in log notes that another staff member reviewing it later understands exactly what happened without needing to ask you.

Ban BOLOs — how moderators handle bans

Moderator-tier staff do not have in-game ban permissions. If a player needs to be banned:

  • Kick the player using the appropriate punishment first (per the Punishment Guide).
  • Create a Ban BOLO in Melonly, fully detailing the offense, the evidence, and the recommended ban duration.
  • A High Rank+ staff member will review the BOLO and execute the actual ban in-game.
!High Ranks must not only accept the Ban BOLO in Melonly, but also actually ban the player in-game — accepting the BOLO alone does not enforce anything. Similarly, any approved unban must also be logged by a High Rank to avoid gaps in the player's record. Not using Melonly leads to harsh punishment — there is no exception for forgetting.

Leave of Absence (LOA)

If you know you'll be unable to meet your quota — vacation, exams, real-life obligations — submit an LOA request through Melonly rather than simply going quiet. Going inactive without an LOA on file is treated as a quota failure, not an excused absence, regardless of the reason.

Operations Tools

Staff Hangout

A break space for staff to decompress, coordinate, and connect outside the pressure of active moderation. Not a place to conduct active moderation from.

  • Reserved for staff who are on break. If you are actively moderating, you should be on the floor, not parked in the Hangout.
  • Official meetings and briefings may be held here. All participants are expected to remain professional and respectful throughout — side conversations should wait until after the meeting concludes.
  • Staff events and team-building activities are welcome, as long as everyone follows standard server rules and staff conduct policy. Disrespect or rule-breaking in the Hangout is handled exactly as it would be anywhere else.
  • Attendance at mandatory briefings will be communicated separately by HR. Missing a mandatory briefing without prior notice can factor into your activity review.

Perks & Recognition

Every perk described here rewards sustained, genuine contribution to GRPC — not entitlements tied to rank alone.

Former Staff

When you retire from active GRPC staff duty, you become eligible under specific conditions:

  • Former Staff — requires you to have been at least a Lead Moderator at retirement. Grants access to the staff chat, preserving your connection to the community.
  • Former Staff+ — requires you to have been at least a Trial Supervisor at retirement. Includes everything Former Staff gets, plus image permissions, GIF permissions, staff chat access, and the VIP role — which unlocks staff-only events and additional channels.

Staff of the Week (SOTW)

Awarded to the staff member with the highest total logged shift time within the current activity wave, as tracked through Melonly. SOTW resets each cycle, so a strong showing one week doesn't guarantee recognition the next. To realistically compete, focus on both volume (logged hours) and quality — a high hour count with moderation errors or complaints is unlikely to be selected even if it leads the leaderboard.

SOTW perks include: the VIP role, GIF permissions, image permissions, and additional recognition perks as configured by Directory.

Former Staff & SOTW

iThese thresholds exist to ensure the Former Staff community remains made up of people who genuinely contributed meaningfully to GRPC's staff team, rather than being open to anyone who briefly held a Trial Moderator badge.
